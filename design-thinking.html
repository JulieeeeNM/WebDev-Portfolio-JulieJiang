Design Thinking Process

1

Empathize

I conducted user research to understand my target audience - potential employers and clients. I identified their needs for clear information presentation, easy navigation, and quick access to my work samples.

2

Define

Based on research, I defined the key problems: how to showcase my skills effectively, make my portfolio memorable, and ensure it works well across devices.

3

Ideate

I brainstormed multiple layout options, color schemes, and content structures. I created sketches and wireframes to explore different approaches before settling on the current design.

4

Prototype

I built a working prototype using HTML and CSS, testing different interactive elements and navigation flows to find the most intuitive solution.

5

Test

I conducted usability testing with peers and mentors, gathering feedback on the design, content, and functionality. I iterated based on their suggestions to improve the user experience.

Key Design Choices

Color Scheme

I chose a primary blue color for trust and professionalism, with orange accents for energy and creativity. The palette meets WCAG contrast requirements.

Typography

Open Sans for body text ensures readability, while Roboto for headings provides clean, modern hierarchy. Font sizes are responsive for all devices.

Layout

Grid-based layout creates visual structure while maintaining flexibility. White space is used strategically to prevent overwhelm and guide the eye.
